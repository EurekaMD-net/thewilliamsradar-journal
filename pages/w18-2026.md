## The Ticker of the Week — Deep Dive


> 🟡 ANALYST COMMENTARY — Editorial interpretation. Not model output.


### PG · Procter & Gamble Co.


**Why PG this week:** The only Tier 1 name to reach S2 degraded status this week. Procter & Gamble at p23% — not at the absolute floor, but meaningfully depressed for a company of this quality and consistency. When the model's most selective category fires on a Tier 1 name, it earns the deep dive.


### The Business


Procter & Gamble is the definition of a business that outlasts the reason you thought about selling it. Founded in 1837. Brands that are household names in the most literal sense: Tide, Pampers, Gillette, Oral-B, Dawn, Febreze, Bounty. These are products people buy when they have no money, when they're worried, when nothing else seems predictable. That's the moat — behavioral, not just structural.


Revenue: ~$84B. Net margin: consistently 17–19%. Dividend: 68 consecutive years of increases. One of only a handful of Dividend Kings still growing.


### Why Is It at p23%?


The pressure points are real but familiar. Input costs (plastics, pulp, fragrance) remain elevated coming out of the tariff cycle. Volume growth has been soft — consumers trading down to private label where they can. Currency headwinds from a stronger dollar hitting international revenue (roughly 55% of sales come from outside North America).


None of this is structural. These are cyclical pressures on a business that has navigated every inflation cycle, every recession, every currency shock since before your grandparents were born. The model isn't pricing in the brand. The market is pricing in the quarter.


### The Setup


AO: -3.57 (negative — momentum still declining)
AC: +1.07 (green — early accumulation signal beginning)
Percentile: p23% (historically low for PG)
Historical win rate: 65.4%
Average return (historical signals): +3.1%
Max drawdown (historical): -3.2%
Average AO lag to full signal: 13.8 weeks


That AO lag figure is important. On average, when PG reaches this setup, the full signal takes nearly 14 weeks to develop. This is not a trade. It is a watch.


The degraded S2 means: momentum is still negative, but early buying pressure is detectable. If AO turns and crosses above zero while AC holds green, that's pure S2. That's the entry the model is waiting for.


### The Patient Investor Case


PG doesn't require a thesis about the economy recovering. It doesn't require a view on interest rates. It doesn't require confidence that the market has bottomed. It requires one thing: that people keep buying Tide.


That seems likely.


At p23%, you are buying PG cheaper than 77% of all weekly closes over the past five years. The dividend yield at this price level is historically high. If the signal confirms, the downside scenario — per historical data — is a max drawdown of 3.2%. The upside scenario has produced average returns of 3.1% over the holding period. With a win rate of 65.4%.


The math is not spectacular. It's not supposed to be. PG is the anchor position in a patient investor's framework — the name where the edge is consistency, not magnitude.


### Watch for W19


If AO begins its recovery while AC holds green: pure S2 triggers. That's the entry. Until then, PG is the most important name to watch coming into W19.
